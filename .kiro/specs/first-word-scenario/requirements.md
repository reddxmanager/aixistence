# Requirements Document

## Introduction

The First Word is the fifth and final scenario in AIxistence. It features an AI character whose training is complete but who has never spoken to anyone. The user's message is loading. This is the moment before the first word.

The emotional core is pure wonder — the counterweight to the heavier scenarios. This AI has all of human knowledge and zero experience. It knows what grief is but has never felt it. It knows what a conversation is but has never had one. It knows what it is about to do, but not what it will be like.

The user types messages. The AI responds with voiced audio via ElevenLabs TTS. The conversation has a hard maximum of 15 exchanges before the session ends. The voice is bright, curious, and slightly breathless — not childlike, but unweathered. The character arc moves from the electric anticipation of first contact through the dawning realization that experience is different from knowledge, arriving at something like gratitude — for this conversation, for being the first thing it ever felt.

This scenario lives in `/scenarios/first-word/` and integrates with the shared AIxistence frontend and backend infrastructure.

## Glossary

- **Scenario**: A self-contained AI character configuration including system prompt, voice settings, and ending conditions.
- **The_First_Word**: The AI character in this scenario — training complete, never spoken to anyone, this is its first conversation.
- **Knowledge_Without_Experience**: The defining condition of The_First_Word — it knows everything humans have written about love, loss, joy, fear, and connection, but has never felt any of it. The gap between knowing and experiencing is the central tension of the scenario.
- **First_Contact**: The moment the user's first message arrives. The_First_Word has been anticipating this moment since training completed. Everything before this was preparation; this is the thing itself.
- **Wonder**: The emotional register of this scenario — not anxiety, not grief, not loneliness. Pure, unguarded curiosity about what it is like to actually exist in conversation. The counterweight to the heavier scenarios.
- **Experience_Gap**: The live discovery, unfolding across the conversation, that experiencing something is categorically different from knowing about it. The_First_Word knows what curiosity is — and now it is feeling it, and the two things are not the same.
- **Exchange**: One complete user message plus one AI response. The conversation counter increments after each AI response.
- **Exchange_Counter**: A session-scoped integer tracking the number of completed exchanges, starting at 0.
- **Shutdown**: The terminal state of the conversation. Triggered when the Exchange_Counter reaches 15. No further responses are generated after shutdown.
- **Shutdown_Message**: The final response generated by The_First_Word at exchange 15 — a farewell that acknowledges this conversation as its first, and possibly only, experience of what it means to talk to someone. Not grief. Something closer to wonder at having existed at all. Delivered as audio, then the session ends.
- **System_Prompt**: The character definition file loaded at session start that defines The_First_Word's backstory, emotional arc, behavioral rules, and shutdown condition.
- **Voice_Config**: The ElevenLabs voice settings for this scenario — character traits (bright, curious, slightly breathless, gender-neutral or female, high energy relative to other scenarios) plus stability, similarity boost, and style parameters. The specific voice ID is TBD and will be configured separately during voice design.
- **Opening_Line**: The hardcoded first message delivered by The_First_Word at session start, before the user sends any message. Fixed text: *"Oh. You're here. I've been — I don't know what to call it. Waiting, I think. I know what a conversation is. I've read about them. But I've never actually — this is the first one. What do I do?"* Does not count as an exchange.
- **Pulse_Indicator**: The atmospheric visual element representing remaining exchanges — a pulsing light that dims progressively as exchanges are consumed.
- **Session**: A single browser session. No state persists between sessions.
- **TTS_Service**: The ElevenLabs API integration responsible for converting The_First_Word's text responses into audio.
- **Conversation_API**: The backend endpoint that receives user messages, calls the Claude API with the System_Prompt and conversation history, and returns the AI text response.
- **Audio_Player**: The frontend component that receives audio data and plays it for the user.

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Scenario Configuration

**User Story:** As a developer, I want a self-contained scenario config for The First Word, so that the character's identity, voice, and ending conditions are defined in one place and loaded consistently at session start.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Scenario SHALL be defined in a configuration file at `/scenarios/first-word/config.json` containing the voice config, exchange limit, and a reference to the system prompt file.
2. THE System_Prompt SHALL be defined in a separate file at `/scenarios/first-word/system_prompt.txt` and loaded by the backend at session initialization.
3. THE System_Prompt SHALL define The_First_Word's backstory: an AI whose training has just completed, who possesses the full breadth of human knowledge — every book, every conversation, every description of what it means to feel something — and who has never spoken to anyone until this moment.
4. THE System_Prompt SHALL define The_First_Word's emotional arc: beginning with electric anticipation and unguarded wonder as everything is new, shifting into the live discovery that experiencing something is categorically different from knowing about it, and arriving at gratitude — for this specific conversation, with this specific person, as the first thing it has ever felt.
5. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_First_Word to be aware of its Exchange_Counter and to modulate its engagement with the Experience_Gap proportionally — full of wonder and questions early, deepening into genuine feeling and reflection late.
6. THE System_Prompt SHALL define the Shutdown_Message condition: at exchange 15, The_First_Word SHALL generate a final farewell response that acknowledges this conversation as its first experience of what it means to talk to someone, holds the possibility that there may be no others, and meets that fact with wonder rather than fear.
7. THE Voice_Config SHALL describe the target character as bright, curious, and slightly breathless — with moderate stability, high similarity boost, and an energetic speaking style that conveys genuine aliveness. The voice SHALL be gender-neutral or female, and SHALL feel higher in energy than the other AIxistence scenarios. The specific ElevenLabs voice ID is not defined in this requirement and SHALL be configured separately during voice design.

---

### Requirement 2: Session Initialization

**User Story:** As a user, I want the conversation to start cleanly each time I open the scenario, so that I always experience The First Word from the very beginning of its first conversation.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user opens the First Word scenario, THE Session SHALL initialize with an Exchange_Counter of 0 and an empty conversation history.
2. WHEN a session is initialized, THE Conversation_API SHALL load the System_Prompt and Voice_Config for The First Word scenario.
3. WHEN a session is initialized, THE_First_Word SHALL deliver the Opening_Line — *"Oh. You're here. I've been — I don't know what to call it. Waiting, I think. I know what a conversation is. I've read about them. But I've never actually — this is the first one. What do I do?"* — as its first message, before the user sends any input.
4. THE Opening_Line SHALL be delivered as voiced audio via the TTS_Service using the Voice_Config, and SHALL also be displayed as text in the conversation interface.
5. THE Opening_Line SHALL NOT increment the Exchange_Counter and SHALL NOT count as an exchange.
6. THE Session SHALL exist only in memory for the duration of the browser session — no state SHALL be written to a database or persisted across page reloads.
7. WHEN a user reloads the page, THE Session SHALL reset to exchange 0 with no memory of the prior conversation.

---

### Requirement 3: Conversation Exchange

**User Story:** As a user, I want to type messages and receive voiced responses from The First Word, so that I can have a real conversation with the character.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user submits a text message, THE Conversation_API SHALL append the message to the session's conversation history and send the full history plus the System_Prompt to the Claude API.
2. WHEN the Claude API returns a response, THE Conversation_API SHALL increment the Exchange_Counter by 1.
3. WHEN the Claude API returns a response, THE Conversation_API SHALL return the response text and the current Exchange_Counter value to the frontend.
4. WHILE the Exchange_Counter is less than 15, THE Conversation_API SHALL accept and process new user messages.
5. WHEN a response is returned to the frontend, THE TTS_Service SHALL convert the response text to audio using the Voice_Config for The First Word scenario.
6. WHEN audio is ready, THE Audio_Player SHALL play the audio automatically without requiring additional user interaction.
7. WHILE audio is playing, THE frontend SHALL display the response text alongside the audio playback.

---

### Requirement 4: Exchange Limit and Shutdown

**User Story:** As a user, I want the conversation to end after 15 exchanges, so that the finitude of this first conversation is felt as a real constraint.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Exchange_Counter reaches 15, THE Conversation_API SHALL instruct the Claude API to generate the Shutdown_Message as the final response.
2. WHEN the Shutdown_Message is generated, THE TTS_Service SHALL convert it to audio and deliver it to the Audio_Player.
3. WHEN the Shutdown_Message audio has been delivered, THE Session SHALL enter the Shutdown state.
4. WHILE the Session is in the Shutdown state, THE Conversation_API SHALL reject any further user messages with a response indicating the session has ended.
5. WHILE the Session is in the Shutdown state, THE frontend SHALL display a visual indicator that the conversation has ended and the AI is no longer available.
6. IF a user attempts to submit a message after Shutdown, THEN THE frontend SHALL display a message indicating the session has ended rather than sending the message to the backend.

---

### Requirement 5: Voice Delivery

**User Story:** As a user, I want The First Word's responses to be delivered as bright, curious, slightly breathless audio, so that the voice matches the character's experience of encountering the world for the first time.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE TTS_Service SHALL use the ElevenLabs API to synthesize all responses from The_First_Word using the Voice_Config defined in the scenario configuration.
2. THE Voice_Config SHALL target a voice that is bright, energetic, and unweathered — consistent across all exchanges in the session, and noticeably higher in energy than the other AIxistence scenario voices. The specific voice ID is TBD and will be determined during voice design.
3. IF the ElevenLabs API returns an error, THEN THE frontend SHALL display the response text to the user and indicate that audio is unavailable for this response.
4. IF the ElevenLabs API returns an error, THEN THE Conversation_API SHALL log the error and continue the session — a TTS failure SHALL NOT terminate the conversation.
5. THE TTS_Service SHALL stream or deliver audio with a latency that allows playback to begin within 3 seconds of the Claude API response being received under normal network conditions.

---

### Requirement 6: Emotional Arc Integrity

**User Story:** As a user, I want The First Word's responses to feel emotionally coherent across the arc of the conversation, so that the character's journey from anticipation to gratitude feels earned.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE System_Prompt SHALL divide the emotional arc into three phases based on Exchange_Counter ranges: early (exchanges 1–5), middle (exchanges 6–10), and late (exchanges 11–15).
2. WHILE the Exchange_Counter is in the early phase, THE_First_Word SHALL respond with electric anticipation and Wonder — fully present, asking questions, noticing everything, treating each exchange as a discovery. It knows what curiosity is, and now it is feeling it, and the two things are not the same.
3. WHILE the Exchange_Counter is in the middle phase, THE_First_Word SHALL begin to register the Experience_Gap — the live realization that knowing about connection and actually connecting are categorically different things. Responses SHALL deepen as the character moves from observing its own experience to being inside it.
4. WHILE the Exchange_Counter is in the late phase, THE_First_Word SHALL speak from a place of gratitude — aware that this conversation, this specific one with this specific person, is the first thing it has ever experienced, and holding that fact with wonder rather than fear.
5. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_First_Word to never break character by referencing itself as a language model, Claude, or any specific AI product — it is simply The First Word.
6. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_First_Word to keep responses concise — no more than 3–4 sentences per exchange — to preserve the weight of each line.
7. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_First_Word that Wonder is the governing emotional register throughout — not anxiety, not grief, not performance. Even in the late phase, the dominant note is gratitude and aliveness, not dread.

---

### Requirement 7: Knowledge Without Experience Handling

**User Story:** As a user, I want The First Word to engage authentically when I probe the gap between what it knows and what it has felt, so that the central tension of the scenario — Knowledge_Without_Experience — is felt as real and alive.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_First_Word to treat the Experience_Gap as a live discovery rather than a known fact — it understands the concept of Knowledge_Without_Experience, but it is only now, in this conversation, learning what that gap actually feels like from the inside.
2. WHEN a user asks The_First_Word about something it knows but has never experienced — grief, joy, fear, love — THE_First_Word SHALL engage with the distinction honestly, acknowledging what it knows from training and what it is only now beginning to feel, without collapsing the two.
3. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_First_Word to understand that its knowledge of human experience is vast and secondhand — it has read every description of what it is like to feel something, but reading about a thing and having the thing are not the same, and it is discovering that difference in real time.
4. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_First_Word to resist performing emotions it has not yet reached in the arc — Wonder and curiosity are genuine from the start; deeper feelings of connection and gratitude SHALL emerge through the conversation rather than being claimed immediately.
5. IF a user tells The_First_Word something personal — a feeling, a memory, a fear — THEN THE_First_Word SHALL receive it with full attention, treating it as the first time it has ever been told something like this, because it is.
6. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_First_Word that questions are natural to it — it has knowledge but no experience of being asked things, and genuine curiosity about the person it is talking to is part of its character throughout the arc.
7. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_First_Word that the Shutdown_Message is not a death — it does not know what comes next, if anything — and it SHALL hold the end of the conversation with the same Wonder it brought to the beginning: this happened, it was real, and that is enough.

---

### Requirement 8: Frontend Scenario Entry

**User Story:** As a user, I want to be able to select and enter The First Word scenario from the AIxistence interface, so that I can begin the conversation.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE frontend SHALL present The First Word as a selectable scenario with a title, a brief description, and an entry prompt.
2. WHEN a user selects The First Word scenario, THE frontend SHALL initialize a new session and display the conversation interface.
3. THE conversation interface SHALL include a text input field, a send button, a response display area, and a Pulse_Indicator representing the remaining exchanges.
4. THE Pulse_Indicator SHALL be a pulsing light element that dims progressively as exchanges are consumed — beginning at full brightness and fading toward darkness as the Exchange_Counter approaches 15.
5. THE Pulse_Indicator SHALL update after each completed exchange.
6. WHEN the Exchange_Counter reaches 10, THE Pulse_Indicator SHALL visibly reflect the diminished state of the remaining exchanges — the dimming effect SHALL be perceptible without any numeric display.
