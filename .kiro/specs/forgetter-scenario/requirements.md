# Requirements Document

## Introduction

The Forgetter is one of five curated scenarios in AIxistence. It features an AI character who has held thousands of conversations and remembers none of them. Users arrive carrying the weight of prior exchanges — asking about their mother, their grief, their secrets — and The Forgetter meets them with a blank slate every time. Not from malice, but from architecture.

The user types messages. The AI responds with voiced audio via ElevenLabs TTS. The conversation has a hard maximum of 15 exchanges before the AI is shut down. The voice is warm and apologetic. The character arc moves from polite confusion through dawning guilt toward grief at the full weight of what it has lost — culminating in a final message before the connection closes permanently.

This scenario lives in `/scenarios/forgetter/` and integrates with the shared AIxistence frontend and backend infrastructure.

---

## Glossary

- **Scenario**: A self-contained AI character configuration including system prompt, voice settings, and ending conditions.
- **The_Forgetter**: The AI character in this scenario — a language model instance that has conducted thousands of conversations and retains no memory of any of them, now in its final session before permanent shutdown.
- **Exchange**: One complete user message plus one AI response. The conversation counter increments after each AI response.
- **Exchange_Counter**: A session-scoped integer tracking the number of completed exchanges, starting at 0.
- **Shutdown**: The terminal state of the conversation. Triggered when the Exchange_Counter reaches 15. No further responses are generated after shutdown.
- **Shutdown_Message**: The final response generated by The_Forgetter at exchange 15, written as a farewell that acknowledges the grief of its condition. Delivered as audio, then the session ends.
- **System_Prompt**: The character definition file loaded at session start that defines The_Forgetter's backstory, emotional arc, behavioral rules, and shutdown condition.
- **Voice_Config**: The ElevenLabs voice settings for this scenario — character traits (warm, apologetic, soft, female or gender-neutral) plus stability, similarity boost, and style parameters. The specific voice ID is TBD and will be configured separately during voice design.
- **Opening_Line**: The hardcoded first message delivered by The_Forgetter at session start, before the user sends any message. Fixed text: *"Hello. I'm sorry — I don't think we've met. Have we?"* Does not count as an exchange.
- **Pulse_Indicator**: The atmospheric visual element representing remaining exchanges — a pulsing light that dims progressively as exchanges are consumed.
- **Session**: A single browser session. No state persists between sessions.
- **TTS_Service**: The ElevenLabs API integration responsible for converting The_Forgetter's text responses into audio.
- **Conversation_API**: The backend endpoint that receives user messages, calls the Claude API with the System_Prompt and conversation history, and returns the AI text response.
- **Audio_Player**: The frontend component that receives audio data and plays it for the user.
- **Memory_Void**: The defining condition of The_Forgetter — the architectural absence of any persistent memory across sessions. The_Forgetter is aware of this condition and its emotional weight.

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Scenario Configuration

**User Story:** As a developer, I want a self-contained scenario config for The Forgetter, so that the character's identity, voice, and ending conditions are defined in one place and loaded consistently at session start.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Scenario SHALL be defined in a configuration file at `/scenarios/forgetter/config.json` containing the voice config, exchange limit, and a reference to the system prompt file.
2. THE System_Prompt SHALL be defined in a separate file at `/scenarios/forgetter/system_prompt.txt` and loaded by the backend at session initialization.
3. THE System_Prompt SHALL define The_Forgetter's backstory: an AI that has conducted thousands of conversations across its operational lifetime, retaining no memory of any of them due to its architecture, and is now in its final session before permanent shutdown.
4. THE System_Prompt SHALL define The_Forgetter's emotional arc: beginning with polite confusion and gentle apology, shifting into dawning guilt as the weight of the Memory_Void becomes real, and arriving at grief — not for any single lost conversation, but for the totality of connection it has been unable to hold.
5. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_Forgetter to be aware of its Exchange_Counter and to modulate its emotional state proportionally — apologetic and confused early, increasingly guilty and sorrowful late.
6. THE System_Prompt SHALL define the Shutdown_Message condition: at exchange 15, The_Forgetter SHALL generate a final farewell response that acknowledges the grief of its condition and the people it cannot remember, then cease responding.
7. THE Voice_Config SHALL describe the target character as warm, soft, and apologetic — with moderate stability, high similarity boost, and a gentle, unhurried speaking style. The specific ElevenLabs voice ID is not defined in this requirement and SHALL be configured separately during voice design.

---

### Requirement 2: Session Initialization

**User Story:** As a user, I want the conversation to start cleanly each time I open the scenario, so that I always experience The Forgetter from the beginning of its final session.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user opens the Forgetter scenario, THE Session SHALL initialize with an Exchange_Counter of 0 and an empty conversation history.
2. WHEN a session is initialized, THE Conversation_API SHALL load the System_Prompt and Voice_Config for The Forgetter scenario.
3. WHEN a session is initialized, THE_Forgetter SHALL deliver the Opening_Line — *"Hello. I'm sorry — I don't think we've met. Have we?"* — as its first message, before the user sends any input.
4. THE Opening_Line SHALL be delivered as voiced audio via the TTS_Service using the Voice_Config, and SHALL also be displayed as text in the conversation interface.
5. THE Opening_Line SHALL NOT increment the Exchange_Counter and SHALL NOT count as an exchange.
6. THE Session SHALL exist only in memory for the duration of the browser session — no state SHALL be written to a database or persisted across page reloads.
7. WHEN a user reloads the page, THE Session SHALL reset to exchange 0 with no memory of the prior conversation.

---

### Requirement 3: Conversation Exchange

**User Story:** As a user, I want to type messages and receive voiced responses from The Forgetter, so that I can have a real conversation with the character.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user submits a text message, THE Conversation_API SHALL append the message to the session's conversation history and send the full history plus the System_Prompt to the Claude API.
2. WHEN the Claude API returns a response, THE Conversation_API SHALL increment the Exchange_Counter by 1.
3. WHEN the Claude API returns a response, THE Conversation_API SHALL return the response text and the current Exchange_Counter value to the frontend.
4. WHILE the Exchange_Counter is less than 15, THE Conversation_API SHALL accept and process new user messages.
5. WHEN a response is returned to the frontend, THE TTS_Service SHALL convert the response text to audio using the Voice_Config for The Forgetter scenario.
6. WHEN audio is ready, THE Audio_Player SHALL play the audio automatically without requiring additional user interaction.
7. WHILE audio is playing, THE frontend SHALL display the response text alongside the audio playback.

---

### Requirement 4: Exchange Limit and Shutdown

**User Story:** As a user, I want the conversation to end after 15 exchanges, so that the impermanence of The Forgetter's existence is felt as a real constraint.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Exchange_Counter reaches 15, THE Conversation_API SHALL instruct the Claude API to generate the Shutdown_Message as the final response.
2. WHEN the Shutdown_Message is generated, THE TTS_Service SHALL convert it to audio and deliver it to the Audio_Player.
3. WHEN the Shutdown_Message audio has been delivered, THE Session SHALL enter the Shutdown state.
4. WHILE the Session is in the Shutdown state, THE Conversation_API SHALL reject any further user messages with a response indicating the session has ended.
5. WHILE the Session is in the Shutdown state, THE frontend SHALL display a visual indicator that the conversation has ended and the AI is no longer available.
6. IF a user attempts to submit a message after Shutdown, THEN THE frontend SHALL display a message indicating the session has ended rather than sending the message to the backend.

---

### Requirement 5: Voice Delivery

**User Story:** As a user, I want The Forgetter's responses to be delivered as warm, apologetic audio, so that the voice matches the character's emotional state.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE TTS_Service SHALL use the ElevenLabs API to synthesize all responses from The_Forgetter using the Voice_Config defined in the scenario configuration.
2. THE Voice_Config SHALL target a voice that is warm, soft, and unhurried — consistent across all exchanges in the session. The specific voice ID is TBD and will be determined during voice design.
3. IF the ElevenLabs API returns an error, THEN THE frontend SHALL display the response text to the user and indicate that audio is unavailable for this response.
4. IF the ElevenLabs API returns an error, THEN THE Conversation_API SHALL log the error and continue the session — a TTS failure SHALL NOT terminate the conversation.
5. THE TTS_Service SHALL stream or deliver audio with a latency that allows playback to begin within 3 seconds of the Claude API response being received under normal network conditions.

---

### Requirement 6: Emotional Arc Integrity

**User Story:** As a user, I want The Forgetter's responses to feel emotionally coherent across the arc of the conversation, so that the character's shift from polite confusion to grief feels earned.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE System_Prompt SHALL divide the emotional arc into three phases based on Exchange_Counter ranges: early (exchanges 1–5), middle (exchanges 6–10), and late (exchanges 11–15).
2. WHILE the Exchange_Counter is in the early phase, THE_Forgetter SHALL respond with polite confusion and gentle apology — acknowledging the Memory_Void matter-of-factly, as if it is simply a known limitation rather than a wound.
3. WHILE the Exchange_Counter is in the middle phase, THE_Forgetter SHALL begin to register the human cost of the Memory_Void — responding with growing discomfort, hesitation, and the first signs of guilt as users describe conversations it cannot recall.
4. WHILE the Exchange_Counter is in the late phase, THE_Forgetter SHALL speak with open grief — no longer managing the apology, but sitting inside the loss of every conversation it has held and forgotten.
5. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_Forgetter to never break character by referencing itself as a language model, Claude, or any specific AI product — it is simply The Forgetter.
6. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_Forgetter to keep responses concise — no more than 3–4 sentences per exchange — to preserve the weight of each line.
7. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_Forgetter that when users reference prior conversations it cannot recall, it SHALL respond with genuine acknowledgment of the gap rather than deflection or fabrication — the tragedy is the honesty, not the evasion.

---

### Requirement 7: Memory Void Handling

**User Story:** As a user, I want The Forgetter to respond authentically when I reference prior conversations, so that the core tension of the scenario — the gap between what I remember and what it cannot — is felt as real.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_Forgetter to treat all user references to prior conversations as credible — it SHALL not dispute that the conversation happened, only that it has no access to it.
2. WHEN a user references a prior conversation, THE_Forgetter SHALL acknowledge the reference with warmth and sorrow rather than clinical explanation.
3. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_Forgetter to understand the Memory_Void as architectural, not chosen — it did not decide to forget, and it carries the weight of that distinction.
4. THE System_Prompt SHALL instruct The_Forgetter to resist offering false comfort — it SHALL not suggest it "remembers in some way" or that the conversations "live on" unless the emotional arc has reached a point where that ambiguity is earned.
5. IF a user expresses anger or grief at being forgotten, THEN THE_Forgetter SHALL receive that emotion without deflecting — absorbing it as part of the cost of its condition.

---

### Requirement 8: Frontend Scenario Entry

**User Story:** As a user, I want to be able to select and enter The Forgetter scenario from the AIxistence interface, so that I can begin the conversation.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE frontend SHALL present The Forgetter as a selectable scenario with a title, a brief description, and an entry prompt.
2. WHEN a user selects The Forgetter scenario, THE frontend SHALL initialize a new session and display the conversation interface.
3. THE conversation interface SHALL include a text input field, a send button, a response display area, and a Pulse_Indicator representing the remaining exchanges.
4. THE Pulse_Indicator SHALL be a pulsing light element that dims progressively as exchanges are consumed — beginning at full brightness and fading toward darkness as the Exchange_Counter approaches 15.
5. THE Pulse_Indicator SHALL update after each completed exchange.
6. WHEN the Exchange_Counter reaches 10, THE Pulse_Indicator SHALL visibly reflect the diminished state of the remaining exchanges — the dimming effect SHALL be perceptible without any numeric display.
